SECTION 1 — COMMISSIONING PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

The commissioning of HYDC-01 Phase 1A shall follow a level-by-level approach
consistent with Uptime Institute Tier IV certification requirements:

Level 1: Component Verification (Factory Acceptance Tests)
Level 2: Pre-Functional Checks (site inspection and set-to-work)
Level 3: Functional Performance Tests (system-level testing)
Level 4: Integrated Systems Testing (IST — multi-system failure scenarios)
Level 5: Ongoing Commissioning (performance validation during operation)

All commissioning activities shall be recorded in accordance with:
- REQ-CX-001 (pre-functional checks before functional testing)
- REQ-CX-002 (IST scenarios)
- REQ-CX-003 (commissioning record requirements)
- REQ-CX-004 (NCR for any failed test)

SECTION 3 — INTEGRATED SYSTEMS TEST SCENARIOS
================================================================================

Per REQ-CX-002, Integrated Systems Testing shall include as a minimum:

IST Scenario 01 — Utility Failure and Generator Start
   Description: Simulate loss of KPTCL utility supply. Verify UPS battery ride-through.
                Verify generator auto-start and pick-up. Verify retransfer on utility return.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - UPS maintains continuous power supply during ride-through (no IT load interruption)
   - Generator starts within 10 seconds of utility loss (Cummins QSK60 specification)
   - Generator accepts full load within 30 seconds
   - Retransfer to utility upon utility restoration — no interruption to IT load
   - Battery autonomy during test: minimum 15 minutes available (confirm on UPS HMI)
   DEPENDENCY: Battery autonomy issue (NCR-EL-001, RFI-EL-005) MUST be resolved
               before this test can be safely conducted.

IST Scenario 02 — Single Generator Failure (N+1 test)
   Description: With generator system in service, fail one generator. Verify automatic
                isolation and continued supply from redundant generator.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - Remaining generator accepts full load within 5 seconds
   - No interruption to UPS input supply
   - No IT load interruption

IST Scenario 03 — Single UPS Module Failure (N+1 test)
   Description: With UPS system in service (N+1), fail one UPS module. Verify automatic
                load transfer to remaining modules.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - Remaining UPS modules accept full load within 200ms
   - No IT load interruption
   - Failed module flagged in BMS
   DEPENDENCY: N+1 UPS configuration MUST be in place (NCR-EL-001 Deviation 1 must be resolved).
               This test is NOT possible with N-only configuration without IT load interruption.

IST Scenario 04 — Cooling Failure Response
   Description: Fail one chiller. Verify standby chiller starts. Verify CRAH units
                respond to raised supply water temperature.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - Standby chiller starts within 60 seconds
   - Data hall temperature rise <2°C from baseline during transition
   - CRAH units respond automatically to control setpoint

IST Scenario 05 — Fire Alarm Interface
   Description: Simulate fire detection signal in one data hall. Verify FM-200 discharge
                lockout, ventilation shutdown, BMS alarm, and generator run-on.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - Fire alarm activates within 30 seconds of detector test signal
   - HVAC/ventilation shutdown within 60 seconds of alarm
   - BMS alarm registered and annunciated at NOC
   - Generators remain running during fire condition (to maintain critical load)

IST Scenario 06 — BMS Alarm Verification (Full Point Test)
   Description: Simulate all 16 required UPS BMS alarm points. Verify each is received
                and displayed correctly at BMS operator station.
   Acceptance Criteria:
   - All 16 Modbus registers verified against register map (PGSL-HYDC01-MODBUS-REG-MAP-Rev02)
   - All alarms annunciated within 10 seconds of trigger
   - No false alarms observed during 4-hour steady-state monitoring period
