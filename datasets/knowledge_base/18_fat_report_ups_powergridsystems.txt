EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Factory Acceptance Testing of the Vertiv Liebert EXL S1 UPS system (Package PKG-EL-002)
was conducted over three days at the PowerGrid Systems Pvt Ltd test facility in Pune.

MODULE TESTED: Representative module UPS-A1-01 (tested on behalf of all 8 modules)

OVERALL FAT RESULT: FAIL

The UPS system passed most functional tests satisfactorily. However, two significant
non-conformances were identified:

1. CRITICAL FAIL — Battery Discharge Test (Day 3):
   Measured autonomy: 9 minutes 47 seconds
   Required (REQ-UPS-002, REQ-UPS-008): 15 minutes minimum
   Shortfall: 5 minutes 13 seconds (35% deficit)

2. NON-CONFORMANCE — Bypass Synchronisation Window (Day 2):
   Measured synchronisation window: ±2.1 Hz
   Required (REQ-UPS-009): ±1 Hz maximum
   While the bypass transfer was successfully achieved under controlled test conditions,
   the measured window exceeds the specified limit.

DISPATCH DECISION: WITHHELD — Equipment shall not be dispatched to site until the
battery autonomy issue is resolved and a revised FAT is successfully completed.

TEST 5 — BYPASS TRANSFER TEST UNDER 100% LOAD
Date/Time   : 06 Jan 2026, 15:00
Engineer    : Sanjay Thakur
Result      : PASS (WITH NON-CONFORMANCE ON SYNCHRONISATION WINDOW)

Transfer Time (Inverter to Bypass): 3.2 ms — WITHIN REQ-UPS-010 limit of 4ms — PASS
Transfer Time (Bypass to Inverter): 3.8 ms — WITHIN limit — PASS

Bypass Source Synchronisation Window:
Method: Measured by digital oscilloscope while incrementally varying bypass source frequency
Measured synchronisation window: ±2.1 Hz
Required per REQ-UPS-009: ±1 Hz maximum
Result: NON-CONFORMANCE

Sanjay Thakur note: "The UPS successfully transferred to bypass during the test because
the bypass source was at 50.00 Hz (stable utility supply). The non-conformance means
that under field conditions — such as during generator operation where frequency may
be 49.0-51.0 Hz — the bypass transfer may be blocked if the inverter output is outside
±2.1 Hz of the bypass source. This represents a potential operational risk under
non-ideal conditions, consistent with the classification in NCR-EL-001."

Sanjay Thakur (WSP Global) formal statement for record:
"The battery discharge test result of 9 minutes 47 seconds is categorically below the
specification requirement of 15 minutes. The test was conducted correctly, under
appropriate conditions, and with independent instrumentation. The result is not
attributable to test methodology error. The battery system as currently configured
is undersized for the project specification requirement. Dispatch approval is withheld."

Dr. Meera Iyer (NxtGen Cloud) formal statement for record:
"NxtGen Cloud Infrastructure Ltd does not accept this result. The equipment will not
leave this facility until a revised battery system capable of delivering 15 minutes
minimum autonomy at 100% IT load has been supplied and successfully tested."

Ramesh Khanna (PowerGrid Systems) acknowledged the result verbally.

NON-CONFORMANCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

NCF-01 (CRITICAL):
Description: Battery discharge autonomy below specification
Requirement: REQ-UPS-002, REQ-UPS-008 — 15 minutes minimum at 100% load
Measured:    9 minutes 47 seconds
Shortfall:   5 minutes 13 seconds
Action:      Battery remediation required before dispatch. See NCR-EL-001.

NCF-02 (MAJOR):
Description: Bypass synchronisation window exceeds specification
Requirement: REQ-UPS-009 — ±1 Hz maximum
Measured:    ±2.1 Hz
Action:      Vendor to provide technical justification. Classify as Major Deviation
             in revised deviation schedule. Engineering assessment to confirm acceptability.

DISPATCH: WITHHELD

2. NCF-02 (synchronisation window) formally addressed with engineering assessment accepted
3. Revised FAT conducted for battery system with all attending parties present
