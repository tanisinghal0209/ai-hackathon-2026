RISK-01 | UPS BATTERY AUTONOMY NON-COMPLIANCE
================================================================================
Risk ID          : RISK-01
Category         : Procurement / Technical
Status           : MATERIALISED — ACTIVE REMEDIATION
Date Raised      : 20 November 2025
Date Materialised: 07 January 2026 (FAT battery test FAIL)

Description      : UPS vendor (PowerGrid Systems, VND-EL-001) has declared 10-minute
                   battery autonomy against a specification requirement of 15 minutes
                   (REQ-UPS-002, REQ-UPS-008). FAT on 07 January 2026 confirmed
                   actual measured autonomy of 9 minutes 47 seconds — 35% below requirement.

Probability      : 5 (Almost Certain — MATERIALISED)
Impact           : 5 (Catastrophic — blocks IST, delays Power-On, threatens client SLA)
Risk Score       : 25 — RED (CRITICAL)

Programme Impact : +8 to +35 days on critical path (depending on remediation solution)
                   Power-On forecast: 25 July 2026 (subject to battery remediation speed)
Commercial Impact: Potential liquidated damages if Power-On exceeds 15 August 2026
                   Client bank go-live: 1 September 2026 — 37-day buffer
Financial Impact : Battery remediation cost: INR 2.8–4.5 Crores additional

Owner            : Aditya Singh (Procurement), PowerGrid Systems (Vendor)
Linked Documents : NCR-EL-001, RFI-EL-005, MOM-HYDC01-013, HYDC-01-PO-EL-002
Linked Equipment : UPS-A1-01 to UPS-B1-04 (all 8 UPS modules)
Linked Activities: A17 (Battery Remediation Design), A18 (BEC Procurement), A19 (Delivery), A21 (Install)

Mitigation Status:
Cure Notice issued 12 January 2026 (HYDC-01-QA-CURE-NOTICE-001).
Battery Remediation Proposal due 19 January 2026 from PowerGrid Systems.
Option A (Battery Extension Cabinets) recommended by Jacobs India — 6-8 week lead time.
Option B (Lithium-Ion replacement) — 10-12 weeks, fire protection redesign needed.
Formal dispatch hold on all 8 UPS modules until battery issue resolved.

RISK-07 | FIRE PROTECTION — LITHIUM-ION BATTERY OPTION INCOMPATIBILITY
================================================================================
Risk ID          : RISK-07
Category         : Design / Safety
Status           : CONDITIONAL — triggered only if battery Option B (Li-Ion) is selected
Date Raised      : 12 January 2026 (Rahul Desai technical assessment email)

Description      : If the battery remediation for the UPS system proceeds with Lithium-Ion
                   batteries (Option B), the existing FM-200 clean agent fire suppression
                   system in the UPS rooms (PKG-FP-001, Kidde India) may be inadequate.
                   Lithium-Ion thermal runaway fires require different suppression strategies.
                   This would require fire protection redesign for UPS rooms.

Probability      : 1 (Rare — only relevant if Option B is selected)
Impact           : 5 (Catastrophic — safety-critical, potential Tier IV certification impact)
Risk Score       : 5 — GREEN (conditional)

Owner            : Dinesh Pai (Jacobs India, Fire Protection), Rahul Desai
Trigger Condition: This risk becomes AMBER (score 15) immediately if Option B (Li-Ion) is selected
Linked Documents : MOM-HYDC01-013 Action 13-03, HYDC-01-FP-SPEC-001, HYDC-01-PO-FP-001
Linked Equipment : All 8 UPS modules, FM200-DH1/DH2

Mitigation: If Option B is selected, commission an immediate fire protection review.
            Dinesh Pai to assess Li-Ion fire risk and alternative suppression requirements.
            This assessment should be completed before any Li-Ion procurement decision.
