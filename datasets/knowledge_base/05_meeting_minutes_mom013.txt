ITEM 3 — UPS FAT OUTCOME REPORT (PKG-EL-002)
---------------------------------------------
Sanjay Thakur (WSP Global, Commissioning Authority) presented the FAT outcome.

FAT was conducted at PowerGrid Systems Pune facility on 05, 06, and 07 January 2026.
Attendees: Sanjay Thakur (WSP), Deepak Nambiar (Tata Projects), Dr. Meera Iyer (NxtGen).

TEST RESULTS SUMMARY:

Test 1 (Visual Inspection): PASS
Test 2 (Insulation Resistance): PASS — all values >100 MΩ
Test 3 (Functional Energisation): PASS — HMI operational on all modules
Test 4 (Load Bank Test): PASS — 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% load steps
   — Efficiency at 50% load: 97.1% (exceeds REQ-UPS-004 of 96%)
Test 5 (Bypass Transfer): PASS — transfer time <4ms confirmed
   — However: synchronisation window measured at ±2.1 Hz (specification requires ±1 Hz)
     This was FLAGGED as a deviation but test completed as UPS did successfully transfer.
Test 6 (Alarm Point Simulation): PASS — all 16 required alarms verified on BMS Modbus
Test 7 (Battery Discharge Test — Day 3): FAIL

BATTERY DISCHARGE TEST DETAIL (Critical):
The battery discharge test was conducted on 07 January 2026 at 100% rated load
(1,600 kW per module) on Module UPS-A1-01 (representative module test).
Measured battery autonomy: 9 minutes 47 seconds.
Specification requirement: minimum 15 minutes (REQ-UPS-002, REQ-UPS-008).
Result: FAIL — Shortfall of 5 minutes 13 seconds.

Sanjay Thakur confirmed that this result validates the concern raised in RFI-EL-005.
The vendor's submittal declared 10 minutes — actual measured result is 9 minutes 47 seconds,
slightly below even the declared (non-compliant) value.

Dr. Meera Iyer (NxtGen) stated that this result is completely unacceptable and the
equipment will NOT be approved for dispatch until the battery autonomy issue is resolved.
Options to be explored by PowerGrid Systems:
   a) Additional battery extension cabinets to increase capacity
   b) Full battery system redesign
   c) Consideration of lithium-ion battery replacement

PowerGrid Systems technical team (Shalini Bose, by phone) acknowledged the result
and committed to submitting a battery remediation proposal within 7 working days
(by 19 January 2026).

Arjun Kapoor (NxtGen): "This is a critical programme issue. If the battery system
requires redesign, we are looking at potentially 6-8 additional weeks of delay.
I want a full impact assessment by next week."

Action: Aditya Singh to issue formal hold on UPS dispatch until battery issue resolved.
Action: PowerGrid Systems to submit battery remediation proposal by 19 January 2026.
Action: Rajan Mehta to update programme with worst-case and best-case recovery scenarios.

ITEM 5 — SCHEDULE RECOVERY PLAN DISCUSSION
-------------------------------------------
Vikram Sharma presented preliminary recovery options for the 18-day critical path delay:

Option 1 (Extended Working Hours): Implement 10-hour shifts for MV switchgear installation
and cable termination activities. Estimated recovery: 5-7 days.

Option 2 (Parallel Commissioning Activities): Begin UPS room pre-functional checks
and cable routing in parallel with switchgear installation. Risk: some rework if
switchgear configuration requires changes. Estimated recovery: 3-4 days.

Option 3 (Temporary Power Bypass): Engage KPTCL to provide a temporary 33 kV
supply point for LV testing while 11 kV switchgear is being installed. Complex
to arrange but could save 8-10 days on critical path.

Combined Options 1+2 could recover 8-11 days, reducing delay from 18 to 7-10 days.
Option 3 requires further assessment.

Arjun Kapoor approved the implementation of Options 1 and 2 immediately.
Option 3 to be assessed by Rahul Desai and presented at MOM-014.
