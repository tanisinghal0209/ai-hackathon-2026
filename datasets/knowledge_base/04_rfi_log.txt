RFI-EL-001
================================================================================
Number     : RFI-EL-001
Subject    : UPS Redundancy Configuration — N+1 Clarification
Raised By  : Aditya Singh, Procurement Manager — Tata Projects Ltd
Raised To  : Rahul Desai, Lead Electrical Engineer — Jacobs India
Date Raised: 08 October 2025
Priority   : CRITICAL
Status     : CLOSED

Question:
PowerGrid Systems has submitted a quotation (pre-order, during tender) in which they
have proposed N-only UPS configuration for Phase 1A citing cost and space reasons.
They argue that N+1 can be retrofitted at Phase 1B. Please confirm the minimum
acceptable configuration for Phase 1A from Day 1.

Engineering Context:
Specification REQ-UPS-001 states N+1 at module level. However, there has been
informal discussion about phasing redundancy to reduce capex. This RFI seeks
formal written confirmation of the requirement to avoid any ambiguity during
procurement.

Affected Equipment: UPS-A1-01 through UPS-A1-04 (Power Module A), UPS-B1-01 through UPS-B1-04
Referenced Documents: HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002 Rev B, PKG-EL-002 scope document

Response (by Rahul Desai, 14 October 2025):
N+1 redundancy is a mandatory Day 1 requirement for Phase 1A. There is no provision
for deferral to Phase 1B. The specification language in REQ-UPS-001 is unambiguous.
Any vendor proposing N-only configuration must be formally rejected. If PowerGrid
Systems cannot supply N+1 within the budget, the alternate vendor (ABB Power Protection)
should be approached. This response has been reviewed by Dr. Meera Iyer (NxtGen Cloud)
who confirms the client position.

Closed By  : Rajan Mehta
Close Date : 14 October 2025
Action     : Procurement Manager to issue formal N+1 requirement in PO HYDC-01-PO-EL-002

RFI-EL-002
================================================================================
Number     : RFI-EL-002
Subject    : Battery Autonomy Calculation Basis — End of Life vs New
Raised By  : Sanjay Thakur, Commissioning Authority — WSP Global
Raised To  : Rahul Desai, Lead Electrical Engineer — Jacobs India
Date Raised: 15 October 2025
Priority   : HIGH
Status     : CLOSED

Question:
Specification REQ-UPS-008 states minimum 15-minute autonomy at full IT load. Please
confirm whether this requirement applies at new (100% capacity) or end-of-life
(80% capacity) battery condition, and at what ambient temperature.

Response (by Rahul Desai, 20 October 2025):
The 15-minute autonomy requirement applies at end-of-life battery condition (80% of
rated capacity) at 25°C ambient temperature. This is consistent with IEEE 1184
best practice and Uptime Institute Tier IV design guidelines. Vendors must provide
calculations demonstrating 15-minute autonomy under these conditions, not new battery
conditions. This has been incorporated into Specification Rev B (see Appendix B).

Closed By  : Rajan Mehta
Close Date : 22 October 2025

RFI-EL-005
================================================================================
Number     : RFI-EL-005
Subject    : Battery Discharge Test at Factory vs Site
Raised By  : Sanjay Thakur, Commissioning Authority — WSP Global
Raised To  : Ramesh Khanna — PowerGrid Systems / Rahul Desai — Jacobs India
Date Raised: 25 November 2025
Priority   : CRITICAL
Status     : OPEN

Question:
PowerGrid Systems has proposed in their FAT programme (PGSL-HYDC01-FAT-PROC-001 Rev A)
to defer the battery discharge test to site commissioning. Specification clause 10.4
and REQ-UPS-020 explicitly require this test to be conducted at the factory as a
witnessed test. This is a condition precedent to dispatch approval. The commissioning
authority cannot accept dispatch of equipment without factory-witnessed battery
autonomy verification.

Please confirm the project's position and whether an exception can be granted.

Engineering Context:
If battery autonomy at site is found to be less than 15 minutes (as the vendor has
declared in their submittal — 10 minutes at 100% load), discovering this at site
instead of factory creates significant project impact: potential re-procurement of
additional battery cabinets, revised structural loading calculations, revised electrical
cabling, and delay to commissioning.

Affected Equipment: All 8 UPS modules (UPS-A1-01 to UPS-A1-04, UPS-B1-01 to UPS-B1-04)
Referenced Documents: HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002 Rev B Clause 10.4, REQ-UPS-020

Response from Jacobs (Rahul Desai, 02 December 2025):
No exception will be granted. The factory battery discharge test is mandatory per
clause 10.4. PowerGrid Systems must include this test in their FAT programme before
dispatch approval is issued. Additionally, the commissioning authority has flagged
that the vendor's declared 10-minute autonomy at 100% load represents a separate
critical non-compliance with REQ-UPS-002.

Response from PowerGrid Systems (Ramesh Khanna, 10 December 2025):
Battery discharge test has been added to FAT programme (Revision B of PGSL-HYDC01-FAT-PROC-001).
Battery test is now scheduled for the 3rd day of FAT (07 January 2026 — one additional day added).

Current Status: OPEN — FAT rescheduled to 05-07 January 2026. Awaiting confirmation
of battery discharge test outcome. If autonomy measured at less than 15 minutes at
factory, additional battery extension cabinets will be required — impact TBD.

Consequence: If battery test fails (autonomy <15 min), equipment dispatch will be
blocked, delaying site delivery and impacting commissioning schedule (currently planned
June 2026 Power-On).
