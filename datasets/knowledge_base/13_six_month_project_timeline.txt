   [→ All project documents begin referencing HYDC-01 from this date]

18 Jul 2025 — PROJECT KICKOFF MEETING (MOM-HYDC01-001)
   First formal coordination meeting. Attendees: full project team + NxtGen Cloud.
   Arjun Kapoor announces the 1 September 2026 bank customer go-live commitment.
   [→ ALL programme decisions henceforth measured against this date]

22 Jul 2025 — DESIGN REVIEW MEETING (MOM-HYDC01-002)
   Jacobs India presents 60% design drawings. Rahul Desai presents electrical SLD.
   Key decision: 2N UPS architecture confirmed (N+1 per power module, 2N at facility).
   [→ This decision will later conflict with PowerGrid Systems' N-only proposal]

28 Jul 2025 — SITE POSSESSION
   L&T C&B takes possession of 6.2-acre Phase 1 site at Devanahalli.
   Survey pegs installed. Temporary access road construction begins.

OCTOBER 2025 — SPECIFICATION REVISION AND VENDOR ENGAGEMENT
================================================================================

01 Oct 2025 — SPECIFICATION REV B ISSUED
   Rahul Desai issues HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002 Revision B incorporating:
   - Clarification of REQ-UPS-008 (battery autonomy at end-of-life condition)
   - Additional FAT clause 10.4 (mandatory factory battery discharge test)
   [→ Revision B creates the stricter battery requirement that PowerGrid Systems
      will later fail to meet]

02 Oct 2025 — ROOF INSTALLATION BEGINS (DH1 AND DH2)
   Structural steelwork complete. Roofing panels installation starts (Activity A8).

08 Oct 2025 — RFI-EL-001 RAISED
   Rahul Desai formally raises RFI-EL-001 seeking written confirmation that N+1
   is mandatory from Day 1. Dr. Meera Iyer responds within 48 hours.
   [→ RFI-EL-001 closed 14 October 2025]

14 Oct 2025 — RFI-EL-001 CLOSED
   Confirmed: N+1 mandatory, no deferral. This closes the informal N+1 debate.

15 Oct 2025 — RFI-EL-002 RAISED (Battery Autonomy Clarification)
   Sanjay Thakur (WSP Global) raises RFI-EL-002 seeking clarification on
   battery autonomy calculation basis (new vs end-of-life condition).
   [→ Response will shape how vendors size their battery systems]

20 Oct 2025 — RFI-EL-002 CLOSED
   15-minute autonomy confirmed at end-of-life (80% capacity) condition.
   This is more demanding than the interpretation used by PowerGrid Systems in
   their initial battery sizing calculations.
   [→ PowerGrid Systems receives Spec Rev B with this requirement confirmed.
      Their battery autonomy (10 min) is now confirmed non-compliant.]

22 Oct 2025 — CRAH PO PLACED
   PO HYDC-01-PO-ME-002 placed with Vertiv India. Value: INR 22.8 Crores.

25 Oct 2025 — STRUCTURAL STEELWORK COMPLETE (DH1 AND DH2)
   Milestone achieved. Activity A6 complete.

30 Oct 2025 — UPS VENDOR SUBMITTAL REV 01 RECEIVED
   PowerGrid Systems submits Revision 01. Immediate issues identified:
   - Modbus register map MISSING
   - IEC 62040-3 certificate MISSING
   - Deviation schedule incomplete (±2 Hz sync issue not formally declared)
   [→ Kavya Reddy prepares rejection notice]

JANUARY 2026 — THE TURNING POINT
================================================================================

03 Jan 2026 — FAT DATE CONFIRMED
   WSP Global (Sanjay Thakur) and NxtGen Cloud (Dr. Meera Iyer) confirm attendance.
   Tata Projects (Deepak Nambiar) confirmed. PowerGrid Systems notified.

05-06 Jan 2026 — UPS FAT DAYS 1 AND 2
   Tests 1-9 conducted (visual, insulation, load bank, bypass transfer, alarms).
   Load bank test PASSES at all load levels. Efficiency exceeds specification.
   Bypass transfer PASSES (<4ms transfer time).
   Synchronisation window MEASURED at ±2.1 Hz (spec: ±1 Hz) — NON-CONFORMANCE noted.
   [→ Sanjay Thakur records this formally in FAT observation log]

07 Jan 2026 — UPS FAT DAY 3 — THE BATTERY TEST
   Battery discharge test conducted at 1,600 kW on Module UPS-A1-01.
   Time to minimum terminal voltage: 9 minutes 47 seconds.
   Required: 15 minutes minimum.
   RESULT: FAIL — CRITICAL TEST FAILURE.
   Dr. Meera Iyer issues verbal dispatch hold immediately.
   [→ Sanjay Thakur issues formal FAT failure notification (email thread 4)]
   [→ This failure event cascades through programme, IST readiness, and commercial risk]

07 Jan 2026 — FORMAL FAT FAILURE NOTIFICATION
   Sanjay Thakur issues WSP-HYDC01-FAT-OBSERVATION-001.pdf at 6:30 PM.
   Dispatch withheld. Equipment on hold at Pune warehouse.

09 Jan 2026 — DR. MEERA IYER FORMAL NOTICE TO POWERGRIDSYSTEMS
   Written demand for Battery Remediation Proposal within 7 working days.
   [→ PowerGrid Systems initiates internal assessment of Option A (BEC) and Option B (Li-Ion)]

10 Jan 2026 — CUSTOMS CLEARANCE GRANTED — MV SWITCHGEAR
   After 36 days, JNPT customs grants clearance for Schneider MV switchgear.
   Panels loaded onto trucks for Bengaluru.
   [→ Site delivery: 15-16 January 2026. Critical path partially recovered.]

09 Jan 2026 — UPS DISPATCH HOLD ISSUED
   Aditya Singh issues formal procurement hold on UPS equipment (Action 13-01).

08 Jan 2026 — WEEKLY MEETING MOM-HYDC01-013
   FAT failure presented to full project team. Arjun Kapoor attends.
   Battery remediation proposal required by 19 January 2026.
   Cure notice to be issued. Recovery programme mandated.
   [→ Most consequential meeting of the project to date]

09 Jan 2026 — POWERGRIDSYSTEMS INITIAL RESPONSE
   Ramesh Khanna acknowledges battery failure in email. Identifies two options:
   Option A (BEC — 6-8 weeks) and Option B (Li-Ion — 10-12 weeks).

12 Jan 2026 — RAHUL DESAI TECHNICAL ASSESSMENT
   Jacobs India recommends Option A (Battery Extension Cabinets). Faster, proven.
   Structural and fire protection coordination needed.

12 Jan 2026 — FORMAL CURE NOTICE ISSUED

13 Jan 2026 — INTERNAL PLANNING CONCERN
