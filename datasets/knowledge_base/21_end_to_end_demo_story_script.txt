THE ENGINEERING STORY (THE TRUTH)
================================================================================

In August 2025, Tata Projects issued a specification (Rev A) for the UPS system. 
In October, they issued Revision B, which subtly clarified that the 15-minute 
battery autonomy requirement must be calculated at the "end-of-life" condition 
(80% capacity). 

The vendor, PowerGrid Systems, failed to notice this stricter requirement. They 
submitted their technical package (Rev 02) declaring "10 minutes autonomy" and, 
crucially, checked the box stating "No deviations." 

The QA Manager (Kavya Reddy) and Lead Engineer (Rahul Desai) caught this and raised 
NCR-EL-001. However, due to commercial pressure, the equipment proceeded to its 
Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) in January 2026. 

At the FAT, the battery discharge test failed catastrophically at 9 minutes 47 seconds.
The client representative (Dr. Meera Iyer) immediately issued a dispatch hold. 

Because the UPS cannot be dispatched, the installation (Activity A16) is delayed. 
Because installation is delayed, the Integrated Systems Test (IST) cannot safely 
demonstrate a utility failure scenario. 

This pushes the project's "Power-On" date from June 30 to July 25, leaving only a 
37-day buffer before the client's non-negotiable bank tenant go-live on September 1. 

A formal Risk (RISK-01) is raised, and the team is now forced to procure Battery 
Extension Cabinets (Option A) to resolve the shortfall, costing an additional INR 
2.8 to 4.5 Crores.
