1.0  PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1  Purpose
     This specification establishes the minimum technical requirements for the supply,
     delivery, factory acceptance testing, and commissioning interface of the Uninterruptible
     Power Supply (UPS) system for the NXGEN Hyperscale Data Centre, Phase 1, Project HYDC-01,
     located at Devanahalli Industrial Area, Bengaluru North.

1.2  Scope of Supply
     The scope of this specification covers:
     a) Eight (8) UPS modules rated at 1.6 MW each
     b) Associated battery systems with minimum 15-minute autonomy
     c) Static bypass switches
     d) Maintenance bypass panels
     e) UPS control and monitoring systems
     f) BMS integration hardware and software
     g) All interconnecting cabling within the UPS room
     h) Factory acceptance testing
     i) Documentation as listed in Section 11.0

1.3  Out of Scope
     Medium voltage incoming supplies to the UPS distribution boards (covered under
     PKG-EL-001). Generator fuel and exhaust systems (covered under PKG-EL-003).

4.0  SYSTEM CONFIGURATION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1  Redundancy Architecture

     REQ-UPS-001: UPS modules shall be configured in N+1 redundancy at the power module
     (block) level. At full design IT load, one UPS module shall be available as a
     hot-standby spare. Loss of any single UPS module shall not cause any reduction
     in available power to the IT load. The N+1 configuration shall be maintained as
     a day-one commissioning requirement for Phase 1A.

     COMPLIANCE NOTE: N-only (2N without the additional standby) is NOT acceptable.
     Any proposal to defer N+1 to Phase 1B will be treated as a Critical Deviation
     and will require written approval from NxtGen Cloud Infrastructure Ltd prior to
     order placement.

4.2  Module Rating
     Each UPS module shall be rated at minimum 1,600 kW / 1,600 kVA at unity power factor.
     Eight (8) modules shall be supplied for Phase 1A.

4.3  Parallel Operation
     UPS modules shall support parallel operation with load sharing within ±2% of equal
     distribution. Parallel bus shall incorporate isolation to prevent fault propagation
     between modules.

4.4  System Availability
     The UPS system architecture shall achieve a calculated MTBF of ≥200,000 hours at
     the system level in the N+1 configuration.

5.0  ELECTRICAL PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

     REQ-UPS-002-NOT: [Superseded — see REQ-UPS-002 below]

     REQ-UPS-002: UPS battery system shall provide a minimum autonomy of fifteen (15)
     minutes at one hundred percent (100%) of the rated IT load. This autonomy shall
     be demonstrated under factory conditions as a witnessed test. Autonomy calculations
     shall be submitted with the vendor technical submittal for review prior to order
     placement.

     REQ-UPS-003: UPS output voltage shall be 415V ± 1%, three-phase, four-wire,
     50 Hz ± 0.5%. Voltage regulation under any load condition from 0 to 100% shall
     not exceed ±1% of nominal.

     REQ-UPS-004: UPS system efficiency in double conversion mode (online UPS) shall
     be minimum ninety-six percent (96%) at fifty percent (50%) of rated load.
     Efficiency at 25% load and 100% load shall also be declared.

     REQ-UPS-005: UPS system output total harmonic distortion (THD-V) shall not exceed
     two percent (2%) under any linear or non-linear load within specified rating.

     REQ-UPS-006: UPS input power factor shall be minimum 0.99 with active front end
     (AFE) or equivalent technology to prevent source harmonic injection exceeding
     IEC 61000-3-12 limits.

     REQ-UPS-007: UPS shall comply with IEC 62040-1, IEC 62040-2, and IEC 62040-3.
     Performance classification shall be VFI-SS-111 per IEC 62040-3.
     Vendor shall provide valid third-party type test certificates from a NABL-accredited
     laboratory confirming VFI-SS-111 classification.

6.0  BATTERY SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

6.1  Battery Technology
     Battery technology shall be Valve Regulated Lead Acid (VRLA), sealed, maintenance-free.
     Lithium-Ion batteries may be proposed as an alternative, subject to submission of
     separate safety documentation and fire protection interface requirements.

6.2  Autonomy Requirement

     REQ-UPS-008: Battery autonomy shall be a minimum of fifteen (15) minutes at one
     hundred percent (100%) of the rated IT load per UPS module (1,600 kW).

     Autonomy shall be calculated at end-of-life battery capacity (80% of rated capacity).
     Vendor shall submit battery sizing calculations including:
     a) Design load (kW)
     b) Battery string voltage (V)
     c) Number of cells and strings
     d) End-of-life capacity derating factor
     e) Temperature correction factor (at 25°C and 35°C)
     f) Autonomy at 100%, 75%, and 50% load

6.3  Battery Monitoring
     Each battery string shall be fitted with a battery monitoring system (BMS module)
     capable of measuring:
     a) Individual cell/block voltage
     b) String current
     c) Float voltage
     d) Battery temperature per string
     e) Internal resistance (conductance testing)

6.4  Battery Room Design Interface
     Vendor shall provide battery load calculations per IBC seismic zone II requirements
     and battery cabinet dimensions for coordination with structural engineer.

6.5  Battery Replacement Plan
     Vendor shall declare expected battery design life and provide a battery replacement
     cost indication as part of the total cost of ownership submission.

7.0  BYPASS AND TRANSFER REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

     REQ-UPS-009: Static bypass shall synchronise with the bypass source within
     plus or minus one Hertz (±1 Hz) frequency tolerance before executing an
     automatic transfer to bypass.

     REQ-UPS-010: Automatic transfer time from inverter output to bypass shall not
     exceed four (4) milliseconds.

     REQ-UPS-011: Manual maintenance bypass shall be provided on each UPS module
     to allow isolation of the UPS for maintenance while maintaining supply to
     the connected load through the bypass source.

     REQ-UPS-012: Bypass source shall be metered and alarmed. Sustained bypass
     operation exceeding five (5) minutes shall trigger a critical alarm to BMS.

================================================================================
8.0  MONITORING AND COMMUNICATIONS
================================================================================

     REQ-UPS-013: UPS shall integrate with the site Building Management System (BMS)
     via Modbus TCP/IP protocol. Vendor shall submit a Modbus register map with the
     vendor submittal.

     REQ-UPS-014: Minimum BMS integration points shall include:
     a) Input mains status (healthy/failed)
     b) Output voltage (per phase)
     c) Output current (per phase)
     d) Load percentage
     e) Battery voltage
     f) Battery autonomy remaining (minutes)
     g) Battery on discharge (alarm)
     h) Battery low voltage (alarm)
     i) Module fault (alarm)
     j) Bypass active (alarm)
     k) Overtemperature (alarm)
     l) Module available/unavailable status

     REQ-UPS-015: UPS shall include a local HMI display with minimum 7-inch colour
     touchscreen showing real-time status of all monitored parameters.

     REQ-UPS-016: UPS shall support SNMP protocol as a secondary monitoring channel
     for integration with the data centre DCIM platform.

================================================================================
9.0  ENVIRONMENTAL AND PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

     REQ-UPS-017: UPS equipment shall be rated for continuous operation in the
     following environmental conditions:
     Ambient temperature: +15°C to +40°C
     Relative humidity: 20% to 85% non-condensing
     Altitude: Up to 1,000m above mean sea level (site elevation 910m)
     Seismic rating: Consistent with IS:1893-2016 Zone II

     REQ-UPS-018: UPS modules shall be housed in free-standing, floor-mounted cabinets
     with IP20 minimum enclosure rating. Front access for maintenance shall be provided.
     Rear cable entry/exit shall be supported.

     REQ-UPS-019: UPS operating noise level shall not exceed 65 dB(A) at one metre
     from any module surface.

10.0 FACTORY ACCEPTANCE TEST REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

10.1 General
     Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) shall be conducted at the vendor's manufacturing
     facility or an approved third-party test facility in the presence of a minimum of:
     a) Client representative (NxtGen Cloud Infrastructure Ltd)
     b) Commissioning Authority (WSP Global)
     c) EPC Contractor representative (Tata Projects Ltd)

10.2 FAT Minimum Test Programme
     The following tests shall be witnessed and documented during FAT:

     REQ-UPS-020: FAT shall include as a minimum:
     a) Full load bank test at 100%, 75%, and 50% of rated load
     b) Battery discharge test demonstrating minimum 15-minute autonomy at 100% load
     c) Transfer to static bypass under load
     d) Return from bypass to inverter under load
     e) Utility failure simulation and battery ride-through verification
     f) Static bypass synchronisation window verification (must be within ±1 Hz)
     g) All alarm point simulation and verification
     h) BMS Modbus connectivity test with at least 20 data points confirmed live

10.3 FAT Documentation
     Vendor shall prepare and issue a FAT procedure document minimum 4 weeks prior to
     the scheduled FAT date. FAT reports shall be issued within 5 working days of
     test completion. Failed test items shall be recorded and retested before
     dispatch approval is granted.

10.4 CRITICAL — Battery Discharge Test
     The battery discharge test shall be conducted at the factory, not deferred to
     site commissioning. Evidence of completed factory battery discharge test is a
     mandatory condition precedent to dispatch approval. Vendors proposing to defer
     this test to site will be treated as non-compliant per REQ-UPS-020.

13.0 INSTALLATION AND COMMISSIONING INTERFACE
================================================================================

13.1 Installation
     Vendor shall provide a factory-trained installation supervisor for the duration of
     equipment installation on site. Installation supervision is included in the scope of supply.

13.2 Commissioning Interface
     Vendor shall participate in site commissioning activities including:
     a) Pre-functional checks (visual inspection, insulation resistance, torque checks)
     b) Functional energisation
     c) Integrated Systems Testing (IST) — UPS portion
     d) Load test at site with available IT load

13.3 Integrated Systems Testing
     UPS vendor shall attend IST as defined under HYDC-01-CX-SCH-001. UPS behaviour
     during the following IST scenarios shall be demonstrated:
     a) Utility failure — battery ride-through
     b) Utility return — automatic retransfer
     c) Generator start and transfer
     d) Loss of cooling — UPS operating temperature alarm
     e) UPS module fault — automatic load transfer to redundant module

APPENDIX B — BATTERY RUNTIME CALCULATION BASIS
================================================================================

     The following assumptions shall apply to battery runtime calculations submitted
     by vendors:

     1. Design load: 1,600 kW per UPS module (100% load)
     2. Battery system voltage: to be declared by vendor
     3. End-of-life capacity: 80% of nameplate rated capacity
     4. Temperature correction: apply derating factor at 25°C ambient (per IEEE 1184)
     5. Peukert correction: apply for high-discharge-rate VRLA cells
     6. Depth of discharge: not to exceed 80% (to protect battery life)
     7. Minimum terminal voltage: to be declared by vendor
     8. Inverter efficiency during discharge: to be declared by vendor

     Vendors shall provide autonomy at: 100% load, 75% load, and 50% load
     All three values shall exceed the 15-minute minimum at 100% load.
