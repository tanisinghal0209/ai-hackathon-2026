SECTION 1 — DESCRIPTION OF NON-CONFORMANCE
================================================================================

1.1  Summary
     Vendor Technical Submittal Revision 02 (Reference: PGSL-HYDC01-UPS-SUB-REV02,
     dated 18 November 2025) submitted by PowerGrid Systems Pvt Ltd for the UPS system
     (Package PKG-EL-002) has been found to contain multiple significant deviations from
     the approved Specification HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002 Revision B.

     In violation of Specification Requirement REQ-UPS-022, the vendor has declared
     "No deviations from the specification are declared" in Section 9 (Deviation Schedule).
     However, technical review conducted by Jacobs India (Lead Engineer Rahul Desai) on
     25 November 2025 has identified the following deviations:

1.2  Deviation 1 — CRITICAL — REQ-UPS-001 (N+1 Redundancy)
     Specification Requirement: UPS modules shall be configured in N+1 redundancy at the
     block level. One UPS module shall be available as a hot-standby spare at all times.

     Vendor Offer (Section 1.2 of Submittal): The proposed UPS configuration consists of
     four (4) modules per power module block in "N parallel operation." The submittal
     explicitly states that "additional hot-standby module capacity is not included in
     the base offer" and offers an N+1 module "separately upon request."

     Assessment: This is a critical deviation. N-only configuration means loss of any
     single UPS module will cause loss of supply to the IT load served. This does not
     meet the fundamental Tier IV fault tolerance requirement and is contrary to the
     client's confirmed position in RFI-EL-001 (closed 14 October 2025), which explicitly
     stated that N+1 is mandatory for Day 1 operation.

     Financial Impact: Additional UPS module costs estimated at INR 4.2 Crores.
     Programme Impact: If not resolved immediately, Phase 1A commissioning will be blocked.

1.3  Deviation 2 — CRITICAL — REQ-UPS-002 / REQ-UPS-008 (Battery Autonomy)
     Specification Requirement: Battery system shall provide minimum 15 minutes autonomy
     at 100% of rated IT load, calculated at end-of-life battery condition (80% capacity).

     Vendor Offer (Section 2.3 of Submittal): Battery autonomy at 100% IT load declared
     as 10 minutes.

     Assessment: This is a critical deviation with a shortfall of 5 minutes (33% below
     specification requirement). This deviation was subsequently confirmed by FAT battery
     discharge test on 07 January 2026, which measured 9 minutes 47 seconds (below even
     the declared 10 minutes). The measured result further validates the non-compliance.

     Resolution Options:
     a) Vendor to supply additional battery extension cabinets (BEC) to increase capacity
     b) Vendor to redesign battery system with higher capacity cells
     c) Consideration of Lithium-Ion battery alternative (requires separate safety case)

     Financial Impact: Additional battery extension cabinets estimated at INR 2.8-4.5 Crores
     depending on solution. Structural loading recalculation and revised UPS room floor
     reinforcement may also be required.

1.4  Deviation 3 — MAJOR — REQ-UPS-009 (Bypass Synchronisation Tolerance)
     Specification Requirement: Static bypass synchronisation tolerance shall not exceed
     ±1 Hz.

     Vendor Offer (Section 4.2 of Submittal): Synchronisation window declared as ±2 Hz.
     Vendor maintains this is "industry standard" and does not represent a specification
     deviation. This position is rejected by the design team.

     Assessment: The ±1 Hz synchronisation requirement was specified to ensure reliable
     automatic bypass transfer under all conditions, including during generator operation
     where frequency stability may be marginally outside utility grid tolerance. A ±2 Hz
     window increases the risk of failed bypass transfer under marginal frequency conditions.
     The vendor's assertion that ±2 Hz is "industry standard" does not override the
     project specification requirement.

     FAT Evidence: During FAT on 06 January 2026, the synchronisation window was
     measured at ±2.1 Hz, confirming the submitted value. Transfer to bypass was achieved
     in the test, but this was under controlled factory conditions with stable grid supply.
     Site conditions may differ.

1.5  Deviation 4 — MAJOR — REQ-UPS-020 (Factory Battery Discharge Test)
     Specification Requirement: FAT shall include battery discharge test demonstrating
     minimum 15-minute autonomy at 100% load. Battery discharge test is a mandatory
     condition precedent to dispatch approval (clause 10.4).

     Vendor Initial Position (Submittal and FAT Procedure Rev A): Battery discharge test
     proposed to be deferred to site commissioning.

     Resolution: Following RFI-EL-005 and instruction from Jacobs India, vendor revised
     FAT programme to include the battery discharge test at factory (FAT Procedure Rev B).
     Test was conducted on 07 January 2026. Test FAILED (see Deviation 2 above).

     Status: While the vendor did eventually comply with the requirement to conduct the
     test at factory (after formal instruction), the initial proposal to defer this test
     was a major deviation. Furthermore, the test result itself confirmed a critical
     non-compliance. The NCR remains open pending resolution of Deviation 2.

1.6  Deviation 5 — MINOR — REQ-UPS-013 (Battery Conductance Monitoring)
     Specification Requirement: Battery monitoring shall integrate with the BMS via
     Modbus TCP/IP. All battery parameters listed in REQ-UPS-014 shall be available,
     which implicitly includes internal resistance monitoring as part of comprehensive
     battery health monitoring.

     Vendor Offer: Battery internal resistance / conductance monitoring is noted as
     "optional add-on" not included in the base scope. The Modbus register map does not
     include conductance measurement registers.

     Assessment: Minor deviation — does not affect core functionality but reduces the
     completeness of battery health monitoring. Vendor to confirm whether conductance
     monitoring can be added to the base scope at no additional cost.

SECTION 2 — ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

2.1  Primary Root Cause
     PowerGrid Systems' technical and commercial teams appear to have submitted a
     standard product offering without fully customising it to meet the project-specific
     requirements of HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002. The N-only configuration and 10-minute battery
     autonomy are consistent with a standard commercial product offering for less demanding
     applications. The specification for this Tier IV hyperscale facility is significantly
     more demanding.

2.2  Contributing Factor 1
     The deviation declaration section was completed without a thorough cross-check of the
     submittal technical content against the specification. The declaration of "no deviations"
     appears to have been a template statement not customised for this project.

2.3  Contributing Factor 2
     Vendor internal review processes appear inadequate for the complexity and specification
     demands of a Tier IV data centre project. Vendor's application engineer (Shalini Bose)
     did not appear to have the authority or knowledge to escalate the deviation issues
     during submittal preparation.

SECTION 3 — CORRECTIVE ACTION REQUIRED
================================================================================

3.1  Immediate Actions (within 7 working days of NCR issue)
     CA-001: PowerGrid Systems to formally acknowledge all five deviations listed in
             Section 1 of this NCR in writing, with classification per Appendix A format
             of Specification HYDC-01-EL-SPEC-002.

     CA-002: PowerGrid Systems to submit a formal Battery Remediation Proposal (BRP)
             detailing the proposed solution to achieve 15-minute autonomy at 100% IT load.
             BRP to include: proposed battery configuration, revised capacity calculations,
             dimensional impact, structural loading, electrical cabling changes, and
             revised delivery/commissioning timeline.

     CA-003: PowerGrid Systems to confirm in writing whether the N+1 configuration can
             be supplied within the existing purchase order price or what the cost delta
             would be for the additional module.

3.2  Short-Term Actions (within 21 days)
     CA-004: Revised Vendor Submittal Revision 03 to be issued incorporating all
             corrections and formal deviation declarations.

     CA-005: Battery Remediation Proposal to be reviewed by Jacobs India and
             NxtGen Cloud Infrastructure and formally accepted before any battery
             procurement action is taken.

3.3  Long-Term Actions
     CA-006: PowerGrid Systems to implement a project-specific QA review process for
             future submittals on this project, including a checklist-based verification
             of all specification requirements before submission.

================================================================================
SECTION 4 — PREVENTIVE ACTION
================================================================================

     PA-001: Tata Projects QA/QC team to implement a submittal pre-screening checklist
             for all major equipment packages. Before a vendor submittal is formally
             accepted for engineering review, the procurement team will conduct an initial
             check to verify that the deviation schedule has been properly completed.

     PA-002: For future submittals, vendors will be required to provide a compliance
             matrix as part of the submittal — each specification requirement tabulated
             with a comply/deviate/not applicable statement. This will become a mandatory
             submittal requirement for all remaining packages.
